TOP 200 Universities Analysis

This is a report analyzing 2019 QS university ranking and only concentrates at top 200 universities ranked by QS ranking. We are highly interested in finding a relationship in these top universities and understands more about these universities.


Does GDP per captia correlates to university ranking?
Does freedom to make choices score correlates to university ranking?

This visualisation show correlation between GDP per capita, freedom to make choices score, and the number of top 200 universities in each country. In the graph, we can see the countries which have more than 5 top 200 universities are mostly having GDP per capita above 1.3. Moreover, the countries which have more than 10 top 200 universities are all having freedom scores between 0.4 and 0.5 which is very interesting.
Overall, we can conclude that the countries having GDP per capita higher than 1.3 and freedom to make life choices score between 0.4 and 0.5 is more likely to have a University that is in top 200.

How does each country's toppest university perform?

This visualisation shows each country's toppest university score distribution. We can see that in UK and USA's distribution, the major scores difference is at citations per faculty. UK has fewer citations than USA's toppest university. This visualisation clearly demonstrates each countries top 1 university's weakness and strength. Even though they are all top 1 universities in each country, there may have some type of score very close to zero. It directly shows the gap between each country's university level.

How does the rank changes in top 10 universities?

The visualisation shows the ranking changes over 4 years on top 10 universities. It is so surprising that ranks 1, 2, 3 remain no changes over 4 years. Plus, the top 10 universities list remains no changes as well, there is no new university that gets in top 10 in these 4 years. These facts show that it is extremely difficult to become a top 10 university, these universities are the pride of the world's education.
There are several interesting points in this visualisation. First, the University of Cambridge ranking is declining annually for 4 years from rank 4 to rank 7. Second, Zth Zurich climbed from rank 10 to rank 6 for only 3 years. Although it seems a lot different between rank 10 and 6, there are over 25,000 universities in the world, rank 10 to rank 6 is still the top 10 of 25000+ universities.
